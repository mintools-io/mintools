Trust is everything in crypto. To get your token listed on major decentralized exchanges (DEXs) like Raydium, Orca, or Jupiter, you must prove to your community that the token is safe.
- **[Revoke Mint Authority](https://mintools.io/revoke-mint):** Permanently disable the ability to mint more tokens. This proves to investors that the token supply is capped and cannot be hyper-inflated.
- **[Revoke Freeze Authority](https://mintools.io/revoke-freeze):** Relinquish the power to freeze user wallets. This is a strict requirement for creating a liquidity pool on Raydium.
- **Make Immutable:** Lock the token's metadata permanently so that the logo and ticker can never be changed, solidifying your brand identity.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Do I need coding experience to use MinTools?**  
A: Absolutely not! MinTools features a highly intuitive graphical interface. If you can click buttons and fill out a form, you can launch a token.

**Q: Is MinTools safe?**  
A: Yes. MinTools is a client-side application. We never ask for your seed phrase. Transactions are securely passed to your wallet extension (like Phantom) for you to review and approve.

**Q: Can I list my token on Raydium or Jupiter?**  
A: Yes. Any token created on MinTools is a standard Solana SPL token, making it 100% compatible with Raydium, Orca, Jupiter, decentralized wallets, and centralized exchanges (CEXs).

**Q: Why do I need to revoke freeze authority?**  
A: Decentralized exchanges like Raydium require that freeze authority is revoked before you can create a liquidity pool. This protects users from malicious developers freezing their assets.
